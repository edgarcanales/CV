Persisting

Persisting at its core is finishing what you started. For some this will be extremely easy for others like myself, it was not that easy. There are so many factors that come into play in order to finish your college degree. One of my favorite quotes of all time is one by Thomas Jefferson. He once said, “If you want something that you have never had before, you better be willing to do things that you have never done before.” Sometimes college will be hard at times. There will be things out of your control that will impact you. Life will happen, you may have a bad break up or oversleep and miss a test. Your grandpa may get sick and you may have to leave in the middle of the day to make it home in time. There may be semester where you didn’t get enough financial aid or that you have to work full time to be able to pay for rent. But there will also be so many good times and so many people who will help you along the way. There will be people who will fight for you and people who be cheering for you to get to the finish line.

Below are some quick tips to help you get to the finish line.

Tips to help you get through college

  1. Create a process that will help you stay organized - there is freedom in planning.
  2. Get involved on campus - You have some unique skills that will make your campus a better place - use them
  3. Show up to class even if you don't have to.
  4. Get to know your professors. They want to help you succeed, email them, and visit them during their office hours.
  5. Network as much as you can. You will never know who you meet in college
  6. Work on all phases of life, mind, body and soul - make time for all of those things
  7. Hang out with people that will make you better.
